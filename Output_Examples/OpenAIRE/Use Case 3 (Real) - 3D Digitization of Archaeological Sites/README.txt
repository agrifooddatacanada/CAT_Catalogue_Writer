SCENARIO
========

A research team at the Institute of Computer Science (ICS-FORTH) in Crete has completed a major digitization project documenting the Palace of Knossos - one of Greece's most important archaeological sites - using advanced 3D scanning technologies.

The team needs to:
1. Document their digitization methodology and findings in peer-reviewed literature
2. Ensure proper credit to all 7 researchers who conducted the field work and analysis
3. Make the digital assets and research discoverable for other heritage institutions
4. Show how the 3D scanning, photogrammetry, and processing work can be replicated
5. Link to the institution's heritage digitization efforts

Instead of separately managing the publication through the publisher, institutional repository, and their project website, the lead researcher uses the Catalogue Record Writer tool:
- Opens the tool and selects "OpenAIRE - Repository Profile"
- Answers a guided questionnaire (~18 minutes) with publication information:
  * Article title: "Three-Dimensional Digitization of Archaeological Sites—The Use Case of the Palace of Knossos"
  * All 7 researchers with ICS-FORTH affiliation (5 with ORCID identifiers)
  * Submission date (December 20, 2022), acceptance date (January 18, 2023), last update (September 11, 2025)
  * Publisher (MDPI AG - open access publisher)
  * DOI (10.3390/heritage6020050) for citation and permanent access
  * License (CC-BY 4.0 International - enables reuse by heritage professionals)
  * File size (12.2 MB, 24 pages) reflecting detailed technical documentation
  * Keywords: 3D digitization, laser scanning, photogrammetry, aerial scanning, terrestrial scanning
  * Abstract describing methodology for capturing large-scale archaeological sites
- Clicks "Export as JSON-LD"
- Downloads a standards-compliant JSON-LD record

The tool validates all creator affiliations, maps the three dates (submitted/accepted/updated) to OpenAIRE date types, and ensures the open-access publication is properly credited. Heritage professionals and archaeologists worldwide can now discover the methodology.
