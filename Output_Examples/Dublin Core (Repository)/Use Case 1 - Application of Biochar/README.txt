SCENARIO
========

An international research team from Estonia has completed a peer-reviewed study on using biochar in constructed wetlands for wastewater treatment and nutrient removal. The research involves 9 researchers from multiple institutions studying how biochar combined with lightweight expanded clay aggregates (LECA) can improve the performance of horizontal subsurface flow (HSSF) constructed wetlands.

The lead researcher (Kuno Kasak) needs to:
1. Register the published article in their institutional repository
2. Track the article alongside related prior work (4 predecessor publications)
3. Document the full research lineage showing how this work builds on earlier findings
4. Make the research discoverable to environmental engineers and wastewater treatment professionals
5. Preserve the citation chain showing which papers led to this final publication

Instead of manually creating separate metadata entries in the publisher's system and the institutional repository, the lead researcher uses the Catalogue Record Writer tool:
- Opens the tool and selects "Dublin Core - Repository Profile"
- Answers a guided questionnaire (~16 minutes) with article information:
  * Article title: "Biochar enhances plant growth and nutrient removal in horizontal subsurface flow constructed wetlands"
  * All 9 creators: Kuno Kasak, Jaak Truu, Ivika Ostonen, Jürgen Sarjas, Kristjan Oopkaup, Päärn Paiste, Margit Kõiv-Vainik, Ülo Mander, Marika Truu
  * Creation date: February 28, 2018 (when research was completed)
  * Acceptance date: May 12, 2018 (when journal accepted the article)
  * Publication/issuance date: May 26, 2018 (when it appeared online)
  * Publisher: Elsevier (Science of the Total Environment journal)
  * DOI: 10.1016/j.scitotenv.2018.05.146 (permanent, citable identifier)
  * Language: English
  * Keywords: Biochar, HSSF, Constructed Wetlands, LECA
  * Format: PDF (published article)
  * Access status: No Open Access (subscription required)
  * Related prior publications: 4 earlier versions/related studies
  * Cited references: 3 key sources that influenced this research
- Clicks "Export as JSON-LD"
- Downloads a standards-compliant JSON-LD record

The tool validates all creator names, maps the three dates (created/accepted/issued) to their correct Dublin Core fields, and structures the related identifiers properly. The full research lineage is now captured - showing which papers this work is based on (isVersionOf) and which papers cite it (isReferencedBy).
