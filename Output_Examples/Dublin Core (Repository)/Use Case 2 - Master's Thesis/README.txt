SCENARIO
========

A master's student at the University of Guelph in Ontario, Canada completes a 300-page thesis on wastewater treatment using constructed wetlands with biochar filters. The student (Martin Garcia) conducted research at the Soils program, combining laboratory experiments with field testing to evaluate how biochar combined with constructed wetlands can improve nutrient removal and treatment efficiency.

The student and thesis supervisor need to:
1. Preserve the thesis permanently in the university library system
2. Make it discoverable in Google Scholar and institutional search engines
3. Track thesis citations and reading statistics
4. Enable other environmental engineering students and professionals to find and reuse the methodology
5. Document the thesis as an official University of Guelph research output

Instead of having the university librarian manually fill multiple database forms and spreadsheets, the student uses the Catalogue Record Writer tool:
- Opens the tool and selects "Dublin Core - Repository Profile"
- Answers a guided questionnaire (~12 minutes) with thesis information:
  * Thesis title: "Efficacy of Constructed Wetlands with Biochar Filters"
  * Creator: Martin Garcia (student author)
  * Creation date: May 15, 2023 (when research was completed)
  * Acceptance date: June 20, 2023 (when thesis was approved by examiners)
  * Location: Guelph, Ontario, Canada (where research was conducted)
  * Department/program: Soils program (isPartOf University of Guelph Soils group)
  * Keywords: Constructed Wetlands, Biochar Filtration, Water Treatment
  * Language: English
  * Format: XML (structured thesis document)
  * License: CC-BY-NC 4.0 (allows educational reuse, not commercial)
  * Repository identifier: 198490/19848 (local thesis ID)
- Clicks "Export as JSON-LD"
- Downloads a standards-compliant JSON-LD record

The tool validates the thesis format, maps the creation/acceptance dates correctly, and structures the departmental affiliation properly. The completed metadata is submitted to the university library's institutional repository.
